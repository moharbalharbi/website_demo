01 Statement A · Practice summary
A · About

An architectural designer working across government, cultural, residential, and competition briefs — translating ideas into places that feel inevitable.

A year and a half inside a planning, architecture, and engineering practice, with the year and a half before that spent at the counter of a bookshop — learning, in equal measure, how to listen, how to read a room, and how to keep a promise. The two together turned out to be the unspoken curriculum.

The work is the patient assembly of concept, development, landscape, and coordination across teams that rarely move in single file. The interest, throughout, is the relationship a building has to its ground, to its programme, and to the people who arrive each day expecting to find a place to be.

Sustainable strategy and user-focused space are not aesthetic claims — they're a posture. The drawings explain the rest.
